## Story Context & Themes

**An American Nightmare** is the second American Setting story and establishes:

### Major Themes:
1. **Tragic Irony** - Max meets Ashley and they connect warmly, only for her to unknowingly eat him hours later
2. **Bureaucratic Evil** - "Section V" allows instant transfer to Vortex for any reason
3. **Surveillance State** - Social media profiles vanish instantly when shrinkies are consumed
4. **Normalization** - Flight attendants casually snack on shrinkies like airline peanuts
5. **Class Commentary** - Even Vortex employees fly economy; company permeates all levels of society
6. **Perspective Horror** - Extended time from Max's POV inside catering trolley and stomach

### Key Story Moments:
- Max and Ashley's airport meeting (flirtation, shared flight, plans to meet for coffee)
- Max detained for cannabis, "Section V" invoked, handed to Vortex operatives
- Max witnesses Thilo Busch eaten by Kelly Lewis
- Ashley asks flight attendants about Max, unaware he's in catering trolley
- Max served to Ashley's tray, desperately screams her name
- Ashley can't hear him (headphones in), swallows him while watching comedy show
- Extended digestion sequence from Max's perspective
- Ashley wonders what happened to Max while literally flushing his remains
- Ashley returns home to emotional family reunion

### World-Building Elements:
- **Section V** - Legal mechanism allowing instant shrinking without trial
- **Airport Integration** - Vortex has direct access to security/detention areas
- **In-Flight Service** - Airlines partner with Vortex for promotional meals
- **Digital Erasure** - Shrinkies' online presence deleted in real-time
- **Prison Pipeline** - Direct supply from prisons to Vortex packaging
- **International Reach** - Operates seamlessly across German and US borders
