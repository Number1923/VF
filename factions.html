Groups & Organisations
Factions

VortexFoods™ Corporation

The central antagonist institution. A global megacorporation with governmental executive powers in most countries. See the VortexFoods™ Corp page for detailed information. Known employees in the series include Laura Giesler (salesclerk), Anna Wendland (delivery driver), Andrea Thiele (senior executive), and Kayla Hayes (Supply Chain Management).

Reinhold & Becker (Consulting Firm)

A German consulting firm that holds major contracts with VortexFoods™ to optimise their logistics systems. Featured in Quality Assurance. The relationship between Reinhold & Becker's executives and Vortex leadership extends beyond business — they are socially connected. When consulting work falls short of expectations, Vortex's "resolution" is extreme.

The Resistance (American Setting)

An unnamed activist movement dedicated to opposing VortexFoods™ and liberating shrunken people. Featured primarily in A Matter of Principle. The group stages raids on Vortex processing facilities with the goal of destroying infrastructure and liberating shrinkies, though the scale of Vortex's operations far exceeds what a small guerrilla group can manage alone.

Paige — a principled activist who insists on non-violent tactics. Her brother's more aggressive approach creates internal conflict.
Dylan — Paige's brother, more willing to use violence in service of the cause.
Jordan — Dylan's girlfriend, hardline and pragmatic.
Seo-Yun Park — an attorney and activist who operates within the legal system to challenge Vortex.

"The entire world knew there actually was resistance. That there still were people who wouldn't eat their fellow human beings, no matter the 'reason'." — A Matter of Principle

Paramilitary Operatives

Vortex and associated interests appear to employ paramilitary forces. In the German Setting, Kim Reineke and Thorsten Henschel are identified as paramilitary operatives. In the American Setting, Mark Campbell serves as a paramilitary officer. The exact chain of command is not fully explained, but these figures operate outside normal police or military authority.

The Von Falkenau Family

An old German aristocratic family that plays a prominent role in the later German Setting stories. Members include patriarch Richard von Falkenau (aristocrat/businessman), his wife Sophie von Falkenau (socialite), and their children Johanna and Konstantin. The family uses their wealth and social position to host extravagant events, including dinner parties at which shrinkies are consumed as part of the social theatre.

The Santoro Family

An Italian family with connections to the German Setting. Includes diplomat Piero Santoro, his wife and housewife/translator Claudia Santoro, and their adult children Alessia and Marco. The Santoros are central to the All in the Family storyline, which explores what happens when family relationships intersect with the predator/prey dynamic.

The Carrendon-Wexley Family

A British aristocratic family appearing in Dinner Party. Persephone Carrendon-Wexley is a diplomat/aristocrat; her daughter Felicity is a student. They represent the British elite's participation in and normalisation of VortexFoods™ social rituals.

The Lindquist Family

An American military family central to the American Setting. William Lindquist is a military officer; Elizabeth Lindquist is also a military officer; Victoria Lindquist works as a receptionist; Hannah Lindquist is a student and the most prominent character in the American Setting. The family's military background intersects with the American stories' themes of authority and resistance.
