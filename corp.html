VortexFoods™ (stylised as VortexFoodsTM) is a fictional international restaurant chain and megacorporation that serves as the central antagonist institution of the series. Operating across the vast majority of countries on Earth, VortexFoods™ possesses executive-level governmental powers in most nations — a detail that underscores just how embedded and politically untouchable the corporation has become.

Business Model

VortexFoods™ operates as both a food service company and a biotech conglomerate. Its core product is the so-called "shrinkie" — a live, shrunken human being, reduced to approximately 1/100th of their original size, packaged in small transparent plastic boxes with individual cubicles. These are sold at retail locations and served as snacks or meal components.

Shrinkies are marketed casually — as low-calorie snacks, indulgent treats, or everyday food items. The corporation uses slogans like "Enjoy your meal" and employs standard retail workers to sell them alongside conventional food. The normalisation of this practice is a key theme of the series.

The Vortex Box

The standard VortexFoods™ product packaging is a transparent plastic box divided into individual cubicles. Each cubicle holds one shrunken person, dressed in a black synthetic jumpsuit that marks them as a Vortex food product. The top is sealed with a plastic foil featuring air holes. The box comes with a snap-off plastic flap for easy opening. Shrinkies are typically sold in boxes of multiple portions and can be consumed alive — most often swallowed whole.

"Shrinkies were one of her favourite indulgences, and she couldn't wait for the pleasant feeling of some wriggly tinies sliding down her throat into her hungry belly." — Take-out (German Setting, Story 1)

Processing Centres

VortexFoods™ operates large "processing centres" — industrial facilities where people are reduced to shrunken size, placed in packaging, and shipped for retail sale. These are described as enormous complexes with truck terminals, warehouses, and administration buildings, typically surrounded by high-security perimeters with electric fencing and floodlights. One of the largest in North America appears in A Matter of Principle, where it is the target of an anti-Vortex raid.

Employee Roles

Employees of VortexFoods™ wear black uniforms bearing the white Vortex logo (a swirling vortex). Uniforms vary by role — retail staff wear standard uniforms while delivery staff wear weatherproof outdoor versions. Known employee roles include salesclerk, delivery driver, and logistics/quality control staff. Employees are depicted consuming Vortex products on their lunch breaks with complete indifference.

Corporate Relationships

Vortex has deep ties to consulting firms, law firms, and political institutions. The story Quality Assurance reveals that management-level executives at the firm Reinhold & Becker have private social connections to Vortex executives, illustrating how normalised the corporation's power has become. Dissatisfied clients are resolved through unconventional means. See the Factions page for more.

Recruitment Practices

VortexFoods™ shrinkies are assigned to packaging based on criteria that include criminal conviction, economic classification as a "net negative" (debt, unemployment, poor prognosis), and social deviance as defined by Vortex's institutional criteria. Some, like Timothy Walker, are formally sentenced criminals whose death sentences are carried out via Vortex. Others, like Megan Sanders, appear to be victims of politically motivated targeting. The story An American Nightmare reveals "Section V" — a protocol that allows border control officers to hand detained individuals directly to Vortex operatives.

Resistance

A resistance movement exists against VortexFoods™, most prominently depicted in the American Setting. Groups of activists have staged raids on Vortex processing facilities to liberate shrunken people, though they are often outmatched by Vortex's extensive security apparatus and paramilitary contractors.
