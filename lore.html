World-Building
Universe Lore

The World

The VortexFoods™ universe is set in a world essentially identical to the real world in most respects — the same countries, cities, cultures, and technologies exist. The key divergence is the existence of shrinking technology, which has been mastered, commercialised, and monopolised by VortexFoods™. How shrinking is achieved is never fully explained in the stories; it appears to be Vortex's proprietary secret.

Society has largely adapted to and normalised the existence of shrinkies. Most people consume them without moral qualms, viewing them similarly to how one might view eating an animal. A minority of people — particularly those with ethical objections or who simply find the concept disturbing — avoid Vortex products, but this is treated as a personal preference rather than a political stance in everyday life.

Shrinking Technology

Individuals shrunken by VortexFoods™ technology are reduced to approximately 1/100th of their original linear size, meaning their mass is reduced by a factor of roughly one million relative to a normal-sized person. They retain full consciousness, intelligence, and sensation. They can speak and hear, though their voices are too faint for normal-sized people to make out without effort.

Shrunken people are dressed in thin black synthetic jumpsuits — a sort of uniform that immediately identifies them as Vortex products and strips them of individual identity. The process of being shrunken is apparently rapid, often described as accompanied by bright light and loss of consciousness.

The "Shrinkie" Experience

Being a shrunken person ("shrinkie") in this universe is a profoundly harrowing experience, depicted in visceral detail across multiple stories. The shrunken characters experience extreme physical vulnerability — every footstep of a normal-sized person registers as a seismic event — psychological horror at their complete loss of autonomy, the terror of being handled and consumed by beings a hundred times their size, difficulty communicating as their voices are too small to easily be heard, and the claustrophobic conditions of Vortex packaging.

"The entire world knew there actually was resistance. That there still were people who wouldn't eat their fellow human beings, no matter the 'reason'." — A Matter of Principle (American Setting, Story 3)

Social Ethics

The ethics of consuming shrinkies is addressed throughout the series, though rarely through outright moralising. Several characters object to the practice: Philipp in Take-out uses vegetarianism as a cover for his discomfort with eating people; the activist group in A Matter of Principle actively fights against Vortex; some characters show unease but continue to participate in the system.

The series uses vorarephilia as a lens to explore themes of power, normalisation of violence, dehumanisation, and the ordinary people who perpetuate systems of harm without viewing themselves as villains. Leonie — Max's roommate in An American Nightmare — represents perhaps the most pointed example: she is a self-declared vegan and climate activist who nonetheless eats shrinkies, having convinced herself of Vortex's talking points about overpopulation and sustainability. In Drive-through, Birgit Kohler articulates a similar rationalisation from a more mainstream perspective: she distinguishes shrinkies from animals (who "don't choose their fate") on the grounds that shrunken people "had only themselves to blame" as felons, social parasites, and basket cases the state is better off without — noting that even self-declared vegans and environmental activists among her students eat them, and that Vortex is widely considered to have a positive carbon footprint. This moral framing is presented without irony, even as the story reveals several of the victims in her box to be innocent bystanders.

Legal Status of Shrinkies

Shrunken people have effectively no legal rights in the world of VortexFoods™. They are classified as a food product. The corporation's executive powers in most countries mean it operates largely above conventional law. Police officers such as Ulrike Grimm and Alfred Hoffmann appear in the series in their professional capacity, occasionally intersecting with Vortex-related situations.

The story An American Nightmare reveals the existence of "Section V" — a protocol by which border control officers can hand detained individuals directly to Vortex operatives operating in airports, bypassing the legal system entirely. Drive-through extends this to prison logistics: Mirko and Sascha's last memory before waking in a Vortex box was boarding what they were told was a prisoner transport to a different facility — a fiction arranged to funnel convicted prisoners directly to Vortex without formal process. Individual police departments also approach Vortex directly with difficult cases: Robert Fischer — a suspected domestic abuser who could never be convicted — was referred to Vortex by his local precinct as a "less conventional" disposal method. Prosecutors may request Vortex take "special care" of imminent releases, as with Francesco Ricci. A Balanced Breakfast adds a further dimension: Paul, a British truck driver on a routine commercial delivery, was stopped at the German border for reasons never made clear and woke up shrunken in a Vortex box — suggesting that cross-border freight operations may provide Vortex operatives with a legal pretext for detaining foreign nationals, entirely outside any judicial oversight and without regard to whether the individual has committed any crime.

Settings

The universe is divided into two primary narrative settings, plus a body of canon guest stories:

The German Setting — The longest-running storyline, following a cast of characters centred around a German university city. Key figures include students Julia Böhm and Bahar Esfahani, Vortex employee Laura Giesler, delivery driver Anna Wendland, and an ever-expanding cast of students, teachers, professionals, and aristocrats.

The American Setting — A second storyline set in the United States, exploring themes of activism, military involvement, and the social politics surrounding VortexFoods™ in an American context.

The Von Falkenau Connection

The aristocratic von Falkenau family — Richard, Sophie, Johanna, and Konstantin — play a major role in the German Setting's later stories. They represent old European aristocracy that has adapted to and embraced the new world order VortexFoods™ has created, hosting elaborate dinner parties where the consumption of shrinkies is incorporated into luxury social rituals.
